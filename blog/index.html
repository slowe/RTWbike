A ride along the coast

Cycling the Atlantic coast of Europe from north to south during the summer of 2017. View the archive, the route so far, or donate to MSF.

Cathedral beach

By Stuart Lowe on Thursday 28th Sep 2017 (18:14 UTC)

I was getting jealous of the motorway this morning as it got to sail along keeping it's altitude whereas I had to go into every ravine.

My left crank started to come off. Not sure why. I tightened it back on, as best as I could, with the huge Allen key on my little tool set.

But, on seeing a bike shop by the side of the road, I decided I should get it tightened properly.

They were cycling from near Nantes, south around the coast of France, Spain, Portugal, and Spain.

We had a nice long conversation, in French, over a late lunch. That GCSE in French has proved useful even if I've forgotten a lot.

Afterwards, we went to a little cafe and they kindly bought me an espresso.

The last 50km has been harder. I had the start of a cold this morning and it got worse so it was affecting my breathing. Nothing awful.

Gijón

By Stuart Lowe on Wednesday 27th Sep 2017 (20:20 UTC)

Having ridden all day over many hills I was disappointed to discover that the campsite I'd aimed for all day was closed. I headed back up to Soto de Luiña. I asked at one hotel. They said there was the Albergue but it was full and I wasn't a pilgrim. Their hotel rooms were €35 but the lady said there might be someyhing cheaper in the middle. The bar in the centre had an Albergue. No go for me. I feel like a second class citizen. They sent me to another hotel. This was €30 so I took it having no cheaper option. It has exceeded my daily budget but he upside is that I can have my first bath in 102 days.
