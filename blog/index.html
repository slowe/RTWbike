In the tyre tracks of Thomas Stevens

On the 130th anniversary, I recreated part of the journey of the first person to cycle around the world on a bicycle. Taking it one day at a time. View the archive.

The end

By Stuart Lowe on Wednesday 01st Jul 2015 (15:00 CEDT)

Had two Brits and then a nice lady from Melbourne ask me if I'm from Yorkshire! Then some Italians wanted their picture with me.

Thanks to everyone for following me across two continents and for tweets of encouragement.

I did my #biketour on my normal bike with only two 40 mile 'training' rides beforehand. I got fitter as I went. Everyone can do it.

Navigating from Silivri to Istanbul

By Stuart Lowe on Tuesday 30th Jun 2015 (21:00 CEDT)

In @stevens1884's day he said the morning calls to prayer woke him at 5am. They seem to be at 3.30am for me.

Ice cream stop. I'm hugging the coast to avoid the much warned about D100. Getting there.

Just been asked for directions! Tried to explain that I'm not from around here.

A bit stuck. Back tracking.

Had my own seven minutes of terror.

A man in a white van stopped to say hello in Turkish. He wanted me to follow him. I think he is buying me a cola.

Istanbul cycling can be quite stressful when the bike lanes stop. I've checked into a hostel and had a shower now so feel more relaxed.

To Silivri

By Stuart Lowe on Monday 29th Jun 2015 (20:00 CEDT)

Had a haircut in Çorlu. That feels much better.

Just had a guy in a petrol station shout "Bradley Wiggins" to me! :)

I know I can only average 6mph in big cities due to lights, traffic, navigation, stress stops. A capital takes the best part of a da

It would be quicker if I knew the streets and drivers but when you are new to them, more care is needed. That takes more time.

The upshot is that I will give myself all day tomorrow to go the last 30 miles. As lots of people have told me, Istanbul requires more care.

Hello Sea of Marmara and... umm... Newport?

In getting my wheel into the Sea of Marmara I also got my shoes wet. At least all the holes let the water back out. ;)

The last proper shower I had was at the campsite in Bulgaria. Checked in to a cheap hotel in Silivri. It is lovely here.

Oh and I was really glad to get off the D100 a few miles before Silivri as its hard shoulder was getting sketchy and the traffic increasing.

6.22pm It is quite exciting to hear calls to prayer echoing out over the rooftops.

Jupiter and Venus were nice and close this evening. Spent time talking to an English teacher.

Camping at a petrol station

By Stuart Lowe on Sunday 28th Jun 2015 (20:00 CEDT)

Aside from briefly being woken by the musical call-to-prayer at 3.30am, I slept about 12 hours.

200 km to go. Not sure if it is quiet due to Sunday or Ramadan.

So far, in almost every shop I've been in the owner has followed me around. It is slightly disconcerting.

Stopped at what looked like a little cafe with some men outside drinking tea (chai). Bought some then the men bought me a second.

We shared almost no language. There were pantomimic gestures, a few words of German, and I learnt the word for flat in Turkish: düz. Brill!

I'm going to go ahead of you. I have better roads and want to make the most of the relative quiet today.

I think the rain clouds are surrounding me.

Asked some guys working at a petrol station if there was camping nearby. They're Chelsea fans and are letting me camp around the back!

Behind the petrol station has better ground for tent pegs than the real campsite I stayed in in Edirne! They also showed me the toilet.

No shower here but then I didn't get much of a shower in Edirne because they ran out of water due to filling their pool.

I am a little short of Çorlu today. I'm going to head for Silivri tomorrow.
